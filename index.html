Burnout Isn't Laziness — It's Your Nervous System Screaming
IG Reels · TikTok · YouTube Shorts · 30–45 sec
Hook (0–4s) — Pattern Interruption
"You're not lazy. You're not unmotivated. Your brain has literally run out of fuel — and no amount of willpower is going to fix that."
Insight (4–25s) — VO + B-Roll
Burnout isn't a mindset problem. It's physiological. Chronic stress keeps cortisol elevated — depleting your dopamine reserves. No dopamine means no drive, no focus, no joy. And here's what nobody talks about: sleep apnea keeps your body in a low-level stress state all night. You wake up already running on empty.
Takeaway / CTA (25–35s)
Rest is not a reward for productivity. It's a prerequisite. If you've been exhausted for months despite sleeping — look deeper than your schedule. Link in bio.
Visual Thinking — B-Roll
Hook: Person staring blankly at laptop, coffee untouched → zoom on dark under-eyes. Insight: Split-screen clock 3AM/7AM. Overlay: cortisol-dopamine loop graphic. Takeaway: Phone down, stepping outside, natural light. Text: "Rest is a prerequisite."
Clip Moment
"Rest is not a reward for productivity. It's a prerequisite." — 8-sec standalone. Works as Reel cover, quote card, or Story.
SEO: burnout recovery, cortisol and dopamine, sleep apnea symptoms women, why am I always tired, nervous system burnout

Short-form Script — Wellness / Mental Health
TikTok / Instagram Reels · 30 sec
Hook (0–3s)
"Your body decides you're safe — or in danger — before your alarm even goes off."
Insight (3–20s)
That first 10 minutes after waking? Your cortisol is naturally spiking — your body's built-in alarm. But if you grab your phone immediately, you're telling that stress response: yep, there's something to worry about. So it keeps going.
Takeaway / CTA (20–30s)
Give yourself 10 minutes screen-free after waking. Just 10. Let cortisol do its job — then settle. Your nervous system will thank you.
B-Roll Direction
Alarm going off → hand reaching for phone (pause mid-reach) → person lying still in soft morning light → close-up of hands around a warm mug.
Psychology applied: Pattern interruption + intrinsic relevance. The CTA is low-friction by design ("just 10") to maximize follow-through.
